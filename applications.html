Applications - Artificial Intelligence

by Tom McGinley

Game Development

Artificial intelligence is used within games to make them more immersive from applications with in-game characters, game mechanics and responsive behaviour between the program and the user. Enemies can adapt to the behaviour exhibited by the player from the difficulty or strategies taken against the character. Methods can involve pathfinding tools, to find routes to different objectives or guide non-player characters, decision trees can also be used to determine the games responses to certain exceptions, e.g. if this happens… then respond with… etc.

Originally video games were designed without artificial intelligence, with gameplay occurring between two human players. The first game developed was by Physicist William Higinbotham in 1958, a game similar to Pong and which resembled tennis, became a part of an exhibition at the Brookhaven National Laboratory. Though as games began to become more complex and advanced the introduction of independent enemies brought a new fascination towards video games.

Automation/Robotics

Robotics is a popular topic when it comes to Artificial Intelligence, from factory applications and production to walking and responsive androids. Within the last two decades robotic applications have seen a massive increase as technology has continued to advance with self-driving cars developed by Google and other manufacturers, self navigating robots that clean and vacuum houses or mow and maintain the lawn.

Simulation

Artificial Intelligence has been used in simulation programs to simulate and calculate different situations and the conditions that can be determined safely through virtual technology.
