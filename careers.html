Careers - Artificial Intelligence

by Tom McGinley

Education and Skills

Careers in Artificial Intelligence requires education based skills around mathematics, technology and information systems however, having logistical thinking and problem solving skills also aids in the process of understanding computer and AI theory. Also having a previous understanding and knowledge of a computer programming language, preferably one centered around AI applications, such as Python, R or even Java. Python is known to be one of the most widely used languages for its features in machine learning and AI algorithms, from boths its simplicity and wide range of libraries dedicated to data manipulation and management.

Careers

Types of careers accessible within the field of AI involve:

  • Software analysts and developers.
  • Computer scientists and computer engineers.
  • Algorithm specialists.
  • Research scientists and engineering consultants.
  • Mechanical engineers and maintenance technicians.
  • Manufacturing and electrical engineers.
  • Surgical technicians working with robotic tools.
  • Medical health professionals working with artificial limbs, prosthetics, hearing aids and vision restoration devices.
  • Military and aviation electricians working with flight simulators, drones and armaments.
  • Graphic art designers, digital musicians, entertainment producers, textile manufacturers and architects.
  • Post-secondary professors at technical and trade schools, vocational centers and universities.

Additional Information

Qualification can be gained from either a tertiary education degree, such as a bachelor’s degree in Computer Science or another related field. Gaining a masters degree or majoring in Intelligent Systems allows further recommendation to an employer of your individual skill set and increases your knowledge and capacity of understanding Artificial Intelligence concepts and systems.

In addition there are many online organisations that offer courses on improving your skills, such as Codecademy, Treehouse etc. These can be in programmatic problem solving, learning developing software skills or learning programming languages/libraries.
